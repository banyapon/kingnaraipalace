Wat saotongthong consists of 2 temples including wat ruak in the south and wat saotongthong in the north. the important historical sites include:

1.Grand hall: the grand hall was constructed during the reign of king narai the great in Ayutthaya period. Inside the hall stands the attitude of subduing mara of luang phor toh with the lap length of 6 meters and the height of 4 meters.

2.Puu building: the building was named after the Persian jewelry traders known as “puutia” or “puuter” the building has l-shaped walls with 2 stories.

3.Kotchasarn building: the building name was derived from “korasan” referring to an important town of Persia. It is atwin building with a balcony in between. It is assumed that puu building and kotchasarn building were residential accommodation for roya guests and Persian envoys or foreigners visiting lopburi in that period the buildings inside the temple have foreign names.

The fine arts department has acclaimed and registered this ancient site on the 2nd august 1936
